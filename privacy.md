Privacy Policy 
Last updated: May 26, 2026 
1. Introduction 
This Privacy Policy explains how your personal data is handled when you use this 
mobile application "Fitomi”. The App is developed and provided by: 
Marius Hartmann Osnabrück, Germany (Contact available upon request) 
By using the App, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy 
Policy.

2. General Principles 
The App is designed with privacy by default. It is completely free of charge, contains 
no advertisements, and requires no user registration or account creation. The 
developer is committed to collecting as little data as possible.

3. Data Stored Locally on Your Device 
All data you create within the App — including recipes, cookbooks, and any associated 
images — is stored exclusively and locally on your device in a local database. This 
data is: 
• Never transmitted to any server, cloud service, or third party by the App itself. 
• Not accessible to the developer. 
• Under your full control at all times. 
You can delete all locally stored data at any time by uninstalling the App.

4. Camera and Photo Library Access 
The App may request access to your device's camera and photo library if you choose 
to add images to your recipes or cookbooks. Please note: 
• Camera and gallery access is entirely optional and only triggered by your 
explicit action. 
• Photos you select or capture are stored locally within the App on your device 
only. 
• No images are uploaded to any server or shared with third parties by the App. 
You can manage or revoke these permissions at any time in your device's system 
settings.

5. Internet Access – Open Food Facts API 
The App integrates the Open Food Facts API (https://world.openfoodfacts.org), an 
open-source food product database. This connection is used solely when you actively 
perform a product search within the App. 
What data is transmitted: When you initiate a search, a request is sent to the Open 
Food Facts API. This request may technically include your IP address as part of the 
standard network communication. No other personal data is transmitted by the App. 
Important: The Open Food Facts API is operated by a third party (the Open Food Facts 
non-profit organization). Their data handling is governed by their own privacy policy, 
which you can review at: https://world.openfoodfacts.org/privacy 
The developer of this App has no control over the data processing performed by Open 
Food Facts. 
No API call is made without your active input. The App does not contact the Open 
Food Facts API in the background or at startup.

6. Data Collected by the App Stores 
When you download or use the App, Google LLC (Google Play Store) or Apple Inc. 
(Apple App Store) may collect certain data independently, such as download statistics 
or device diagnostics. This data collection is governed by their respective privacy 
policies: 
• Google Play: https://policies.google.com/privacy 
• Apple App Store: https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy 
The developer has no access to personally identifiable information collected by these 
platforms.

7. No Analytics or Crash Reporting 
The App does not use any analytics tools, crash reporting services, or tracking SDKs. 
The developer does not collect any usage data, diagnostic data, or behavioral data 
through the App.

8. No Advertising 
The App contains no advertisements and no advertising SDKs. No data is shared with 
advertising networks.

9. Legal Basis for Data Processing (GDPR) 
For users in the European Union, the following applies under the General Data 
Protection Regulation (GDPR): 
• Local data storage: The legal basis is the performance of a contract / provision 
of the App's core functionality (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR). You are always in full control 
of your locally stored data. 
• Open Food Facts API (IP address transmission): The legal basis is your 
consent expressed through your active action of performing a search (Art. 6(1)(a) 
GDPR), as well as the legitimate interest in providing the requested functionality 
(Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).

10. Data Transfers Outside the EU/EEA 
When you use the Open Food Facts API, your request (including your IP address) may 
be processed on servers that could be located outside the European Economic Area. 
Please refer to the Open Food Facts privacy policy for details on their data transfers and 
safeguards.

11. Your Rights Under the GDPR 
As a resident of the European Union, you have the following rights regarding your 
personal data: 
• Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR): You may request information about the personal 
data processed about you. 
• Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR): You may request correction of inaccurate 
data. 
• Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR): You may request deletion of your data. For 
locally stored app data, you can delete it directly at any time via the App or by 
uninstalling the App. 
• Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR): You may request that 
processing be restricted. 
• Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR): You may request a copy of your data in 
a structured format. 
• Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR): You may object to certain processing activities. 
• Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3) GDPR): Where processing is based on 
consent, you may withdraw it at any time. 
To exercise any of these rights, please contact the developer. You also have the right to 
lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. The competent authority for residents of 
Germany is: 
Die Bundesbeauftragte für den Datenschutz und die Informationsfreiheit (BfDI) 
https://www.bfdi.bund.de

12. Data Security 
All data you create is stored locally on your device and is protected by your device's 
built-in security mechanisms (e.g., device encryption, passcode/biometric protection). 
The developer does not have access to your device or the data stored on it.

The App is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or 16 in certain EU member 
states). The developer does not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you 
believe a child has provided personal data through the App, please contact the 
developer so appropriate action can be taken.
